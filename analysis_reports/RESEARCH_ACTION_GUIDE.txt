SECTION 5: DNA TESTING RECOMMENDATIONS
==============================================================================================================

GENETIC GENEALOGY:
DNA testing can CONFIRM or REFUTE genealogical connections, especially for distant ancestors.

RECOMMENDED TESTS:

1. AncestryDNA
   → Largest database (good for finding relatives)
   → Ethnicity estimate (should show strong Scottish/Irish)
   → Matches you to relatives who tested
   
2. 23andMe
   → Health reports (relevant given infant deaths in family)
   → Ancestry reports
   → Smaller database than Ancestry
   
3. FamilyTreeDNA (Y-DNA test)
   → Y-DNA traces DIRECT MALE LINE (father→father→father)
   → Can confirm Montgomery lineage back to medieval period
   → Haplogroup might match other Montgomerys
   
WHAT YOU'LL LEARN:
• Confirm Scottish/Irish ancestry percentage
• Connect with distant Montgomery cousins
• Verify specific branches of the tree
• Identify any "non-paternity events" (adoptions, misattributed paternity)
• Health info (genetic conditions that might explain infant deaths)

EXPECTED RESULTS:
Based on your tree, you should be:
  → 40-60% Scottish/Irish
  → 20-40% English (Norman ancestry)
  → 10-20% Other Northwestern European
  → Y-DNA haplogroup: Likely R1b (common in Norman/Scottish nobility)
